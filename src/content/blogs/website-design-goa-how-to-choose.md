If you run a business in Goa—whether in Panjim, Margao, Calangute, or a quieter inland town—you probably know you need a proper website. The harder part is picking someone who actually delivers.

Here is a practical checklist so you can compare options without getting lost in jargon.

## Start with live examples, not promises

Ask for **three to five live websites** they built end-to-end. Open them on your phone.

- Do pages load quickly on 4G?
- Is text readable without zooming?
- Can you find phone, WhatsApp, or booking within one scroll?

If an agency only shows mockups or templates they did not ship, treat that as a yellow flag.

## Website design in Goa should be mobile-first by default

Most local searches for hotels, salons, clinics, and services happen on phones—often by tourists or people comparing options on the move.

If the designer’s own portfolio feels cramped or slow on mobile, your site will likely feel the same.

## Clarify scope before talking price

Be clear about:

- Number of pages (home, services, about, contact, gallery).
- Whether you need **booking**, **WhatsApp click-to-chat**, or **Google Maps** embedded.
- Who writes the copy—you or them.

A fixed quote beats “we will figure it out later,” which often ends with surprise invoices.

## Ask about timeline and revisions

For a typical small-business site, **two to four weeks** is reasonable if content is ready.

Ask how many **design rounds** are included and how change requests work after launch.

## SEO basics matter for “near me” searches

You do not need a 50-page blog on day one. You do need:

- Clean page titles and descriptions.
- One clear topic per page.
- Fast loading and a Google Business Profile that matches your name, phone, and city.

Whoever handles your **website design in Goa** should at least set technical foundations so you can grow into local SEO later.

## Red flags

- No contract or written scope.
- “We will submit to Google” without explaining what that means.
- Ownership of domain or hosting locked to only them.

Take your time. The right partner saves money because you launch once—instead of rebuilding six months later.
